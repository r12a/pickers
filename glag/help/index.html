This Unicode character app allows you to produce or analyse runs of Template text using the Template script. Character apps are especially useful for people who don't know a script well, as characters are displayed in ways that aid identification.

If something is broken or missing raise an issue. For release notes see the Github commit list. The pictures of the UI used in this page are taken from a variety of character apps.

Inputting characters

The app includes characters in the Unicode Javanese block that are used for writing text in the Javanese language.

To analyse text, copy & paste it into the large text area and select the desired control(s).

To produce Javanese text in the text area, do one of the following:

  • Type on your keyboard, using type-assist. A quick way to input text from ASCII characters on your keyboard, based on a Latin transcription.
  • Open the shape-based input, and click on similar shapes to highlight characters you may be looking for. Good when you don't know the orthography well.
  • Click on the characters in the selection area. The blocks are arranged to assist with input, and groups of characters are arranged within a block phonetically.

The above are described in more detail elsewhere on this page.

Click on FORMAT to access and input invisible formatting characters used to control direction, joining forms, etc.

Click on SUBJ to show subjoined forms of consonants. This may be useful for spotting shapes. When you click on these, a pangkon is automatically inserted into the text area ‚Äì there is no need to click on that separately.

Transliterate. Converts the contents of the text area to a Latin transliteration that provides a one-to-one correspondence between Adlam and Latin symbols.

The transliteration scheme was developed for this character app, and, where possible, uses symbols that represent sounds typically associated with their Adlam equivalents, rather than the sometimes fairly arbitrary selection of diacritics found in other systems. Of course, the complexities of mapping graphemes to sounds means that this is far from a real phonetic transcription, and it doesn't show vowels for unvowelled text, but the process is reversible (unlike most transcriptions).

Transliterations produced this way are particularly useful for understanding the composition of text in scripts, where the individual parts are not well separated in the text.

Upper-/Lower-case. Converts text to upper- or lowercase.

Transcribe to IPA. Converts Adlam text to an IPA transcription. This is only a rough, phonemic transcription, and the result should be checked before use.

Transcribe to Latin. Converts Adlam text to the Latin orthography for Pular. This will generally work, but may not capture some edge cases. The result should therefore always be checked.

Vocab to Example. If you have a line of vocab data, like those shown above, this will convert it to an example.
